# PROJECT LIMINAL — APPROVED DECISIONS

This document records decisions explicitly approved by the Creative Director.

---

## Decision 001 — Limited Physical Inventory

**Status:** Approved

Players use a limited physical inventory consisting of:

- Two physical hand slots.
- Quick-access body slots.
- Limited backpack storage.

The inventory exists to encourage immersion, cooperation, meaningful resource decisions, and physical interaction.

---

## Decision 002 — Physical Immersion Principle

**Status:** Approved

Interactions should be performed physically wherever practical.

The game should favour:

- Reaching.
- Grabbing.
- Pulling.
- Pushing.
- Rotating.
- Inserting.
- Manipulating.
- Physically using tools.

Examples include:

- Physically flicking light switches.
- Physically opening doors.
- Physically opening drawers.
- Physically using crowbars.
- Physically manipulating lockpicks.

Interaction complexity should be proportional to the importance, tension, and frequency of the interaction.

---

## Decision 003 — Hunger System

**Status:** Approved

Hunger gradually decreases over time.

Food is discovered throughout the environment and restores hunger.

Hunger creates long-term survival pressure without becoming a repetitive maintenance task.

Low hunger may reduce physical effectiveness and negatively affect sanity.

Eating provides a small sanity benefit.

---

## Decision 004 — Hybrid Sanity System

**Status:** Approved

Some sanity effects are private to the affected player, while others are shared across the multiplayer session.

Private effects may include:

- Hallucinated sounds.
- False figures.
- False entities.
- Environmental distortions.
- False perceptions.

Shared effects may include:

- Environmental changes.
- Group audio events.
- Real entity manifestations.

Sanity effects should be governed by understandable rules influenced by sanity level, environment, entity state, player condition, and controlled randomness.

---

## Decision 005 — Injury and Incapacitation System

**Status:** Approved

Physical danger is represented through an injury and condition system rather than a traditional abstract health bar.

Different injuries may affect:

- Movement.
- Stamina.
- Interaction.
- Psychological stability.

Severe injuries may result in incapacitation.

Other players may be able to:

- Rescue.
- Treat.
- Carry.
- Drag.
- Assist.

Death will be designed separately.

---

## Decision 006 — Connected World Structure

**Status:** Approved

The world consists of a procedurally generated network of connected spaces.

The host-generated world graph determines large-scale structure.

Individual spaces may contain their own:

- Procedural layouts.
- Landmarks.
- Resources.
- Events.
- Entity activity.

---

## Decision 007 — Physical and Special World Transitions

**Status:** Approved

Players move between connected spaces through a combination of:

- Physical environmental transitions.
- Special transition points.

Physical transitions include:

- Doors.
- Corridors.
- Tunnels.
- Staircases.
- Ladders.

Special transitions include:

- Elevators.
- Trains.
- Strange doorways.
- Reality anomalies.

---

## Decision 008 — Hybrid Navigation

**Status:** Approved

Players begin with limited knowledge of their surroundings.

They may gradually gain access to:

- Physical maps.
- Compasses.
- Electronic navigation devices.
- Environmental landmarks.
- Other navigational information.

Navigation tools should assist players without completely eliminating uncertainty.

Maps and navigational information may be incomplete, outdated, unreliable, or anomalous.

---

## Decision 009 — Environmental Rules

**Status:** Approved

Major spaces may possess distinct environmental rules affecting:

- Navigation.
- Lighting.
- Sound.
- Entity behaviour.
- Sanity.
- Resources.
- Interaction.
- Other systems.

Rules should primarily be discovered through exploration and consequences rather than explicit tutorials.

Rules should remain consistent within their intended context.

Deliberate changes should have an understandable cause.
