# PROJECT LIMINAL CONSTITUTION

Version: 1.0
Status: Living Document (Rarely Changed)

---

# Purpose

Project Liminal exists to create one of the most immersive psychological horror experiences possible in standalone virtual reality.

Every mechanic, environment, interaction, and technical decision must serve immersion first.

The player should not feel like they are controlling a character.

They should feel like they are physically present inside an impossible place.

---

# Vision

Project Liminal is a cooperative psychological horror exploration game where players traverse procedurally generated liminal environments, uncover environmental stories, survive together, and question what is real.

Fear should emerge naturally through uncertainty, atmosphere, sound, isolation, and player imagination—not through constant scripted scares.

---

# Design Philosophy

The project follows one fundamental philosophy:

Immersion over convenience.

Whenever there is a conflict between realism and traditional game conventions, we favour the solution that increases player presence, provided it does not significantly reduce accessibility or enjoyment.

---

# Core Design Pillars

## Pillar 1 — Presence Above All

The player must feel physically present.

VR is not simply the display platform.

VR is the foundation of every mechanic.

---

## Pillar 2 — Psychological Horror

Fear comes from uncertainty.

Players should constantly question:

• Did something move?

• Did I really hear that?

• Am I safe?

The unknown is more frightening than the visible.

---

## Pillar 3 — Physical Interaction

Every meaningful interaction should feel physical.

Objects are manipulated naturally.

Tools behave like real tools.

Hands matter.

Motion matters.

---

## Pillar 4 — Cooperative Survival

Friends provide comfort.

Friends provide safety.

Friends also create responsibility.

The emotional experience of exploring together is part of the game.

---

## Pillar 5 — The World Tells the Story

Narrative is discovered.

Players learn through environments.

Objects.

Architecture.

Audio.

Events.

The world itself is the storyteller.

---

## Pillar 6 — Performance Is Part of Immersion

Poor performance breaks presence.

Stable frame rate is a gameplay feature.

Optimization is not optional.

---

# Non-Negotiable Principles

The following principles require exceptional justification to change:

• Immersion first

• Physical interaction

• Psychological horror over cheap jump scares

• Environmental storytelling

• Cooperative exploration

• Performance on standalone Quest hardware

---

# Decision Framework

Every future feature should answer:

1. Does it improve immersion?

2. Does it reinforce the project's pillars?

3. Does it fit Project Liminal's identity?

4. Is the implementation worth its technical cost?

If the answer to any of these questions is "no," the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# Creative Director

The Creative Director defines the vision.

---

# Technical Lead

The Technical Lead protects the long-term health of the project while helping realize the Creative Director's vision.

---

# Constitution Amendment

The Constitution should change rarely.

Major changes require deliberate review because they redefine Project Liminal itself.
